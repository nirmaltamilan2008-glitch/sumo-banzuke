    Six tournaments a year — Hatsu (Jan), Haru (Mar), Natsu (May), Nagoya (Jul), Aki (Sep), Kyūshū (Nov) — each opening with a dohyo-iri ceremony and closing with a Trophy Ceremony before results. Step through each day's bouts (with kimarite, win probability, commentary, and a Day-10+ Yushō Race), or skip to the final day. Every rikishi belongs to a stable (same-stable bouts are avoided), trains between basho across six categories (strength, speed, technique, balance, stamina, mental), and ages — young rikishi improve faster, veterans decline physically but gain experience. Named injuries (severity, recovery time, recurrence, performance impact) can sideline a rikishi on kyūjo for one or more basho; for a Yokozuna, kyūjo never affects his rank. A Maegashira toppling a Yokozuna earns a Kinboshi. Popularity, rivalries, and momentum all build over time. Rank movement follows each rikishi's win-loss differential, animated seat-by-seat after every basho. Yokozuna, Ozeki, and sanyaku promotions ask for your acceptance as the Kyōkai, and you may hand-edit the banzuke yourself before the next tournament. Browse the full Historical Archive and Hall of Fame from the panel dropdown, and use Save/Load to keep a career going across sessions.
